Annotated resources I've actually read. One-sentence annotations
are my own take, not summaries.

### AI governance & policy
- **[80,000 Hours AI Risk Essential Reading](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/artificial-intelligence/)** — The best single entry point; dense but worth the time.
- **[CSET AI Policy Primer](https://cset.georgetown.edu/)** — Strong on the institutional landscape; weaker on technical detail.
- **[NIST AI RMF](https://www.nist.gov/artificial-intelligence/ai-risk-management-framework)** — Read this to understand what "responsible AI" means to federal agencies.

### Technical foundations
- **[The Alignment Problem from a Deep Learning Perspective](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.00626)** — Best paper for a policy-focused reader who wants genuine technical grounding.

### Compute & chips
- **[CSET Compute Governance Report](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/ai-chips-and-the-china-competition/)** — The foundational text for compute governance as a policy lever.
